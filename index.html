John Wilkes Booth

Early Life

John W. Booth was bron on May 10th 1838 in a small place near Bel Air, Maryland. He was the second youngest 10 other siblings in his family. Him and his family all lived on a farm (they owned slaves) and spent most of his tie either at school or on the farm. Booth had attended two different schools, first was Milton boarding school and later came St. Tomothy Hall. He was a very well educated child but he wanted to follow in the footsteps of his father of being an actor.

Acting Career

At the age of 17, Booth had made his first debut in Baltimore with a role in Shakespeare's play, "Richard III". His acting was such a hit that he was invited to tour with an acting company all around virgina. In 1862 he had a debut for the play, "Richard III", in and the people had described him as a "Vertiable sensation". But due to an Illness in 1863, Booth had to take a break from acting. Before giving the Gettysburg Address, President Lincoln had gone and watched one of Booths plays called, "The Marble Heart".

Politics and Conspiracies

In 1850 Booth had joined a group called the, "Know-Nothing Party", and their general focus was to aim at limiting immigration in the U.S. In 1859 Booth joined the virgina military to support slavery in the south. During the Civil war, Booth had served as a secret agent for the confederacy and became part of a conspiracies to kidnap the president. Their goal was to either get peace or get him to release the confederate soldiers as a ransom.

Lincoln's Assassination

Booth saw the plans failed to kidnap the president because he was not going to be seated where they expected, so Booth took matters into his own hand and were a bit extreme. On April 14th, 1865 at 10pm in the Fords Threater, President Licoln was assassinated by John W. Booth. During the play("Our American Cousin") Booth went up and shot Lincoln in the back of the head. He thought that if he shot the president it would cause an uprise in the south for the war. After shooting Lincoln, Booth got onto the stage and shouted, "Sic sempar tyrannis! (Thusever to tyrants!) The south is avenged!". After yelling that, he jumped off the stage breaking his leg but still managing to get onto his horse.

Death

He had died in Port Royale, Virgina on Richard H. Garrett's farm. Him and his crew were attempting to hide in the barn, but once they were found Booth refused to surrender. This followed with his presuers setting the barn on fire. As the barn fire was going on, Booth was shot in the neck and later died out on the pourch 3 hours later. It is siad that the person who shot him (Thomas P. 'Boston' Corbett) was intending to shoot him in the arm and paralyze him.
